Step One When & Where?

Feeling festive and ambitious? Go ahead and plan out a date at the park or birthday party for Milo!

Whether you want to initiate a hangout or join the action, the most important part of organization involves the when and where.

So go ahead and fill out those times slots, dates, and location to get started! Then we can show you what’s the next big event happening around your area.

Step Two the pup deets!

Here’s where the matchmaking starts.

Completely head-over-heels for your furry legged companion and know all of their likes and dislikes? Favorite games, snacks, play-style, and usual behavior towards other dogs big and small? Then tell us all about it! We’d love to get a chance to know Milo more. The more details and information you can share, the better! It’ll help us find the best potential matches with other pups out there.

Think of it like creating a Tinder profile for dogs.

Steve’s just got a photo of him and a stick... Is he down to play and want to share or is he just showing off? Gimme some more deets, Steve!

But wow, Luna enjoys sniffing flowers and likes to take a walk in the park with some company! Sounds like my idea of fun!

Step Three Rendez-vous!

Now that you’ve gotten your potential matches, it’s time to go on a first date!

Set out some time to meet up before any major events or hangouts to do a first-time introduction. It’s important to get to know each other and see if the vibes are vibing. Even if I like to play frisbee with my buddy Rex, it doesn't mean that I’ll want to play frisbee with just about anyone!

Let’s get to know each other first before planning out an entire day at the Bois de Vincennes together.

Remember, this applies to both hoomans and doggos alike ;)

If all goes well, then woof woof hurray!

Félicitations, you’ve just made a paw’some pal!
